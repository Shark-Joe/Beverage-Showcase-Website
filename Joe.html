What is Coffee Bean?

Coffee beans are actually the seeds found inside the fruit, often referred to as coffee cherries. After the cherries are harvested, they go through a process to remove the outer skin and pulp, revealing the green coffee beans. These beans are then dried, sorted, and usually roasted to enhance their flavor.

The roasting process is a crucial step in coffee production. It transforms the green coffee beans into the aromatic and flavorful brown beans we're familiar with. Roasting brings out the unique characteristics of different coffee varieties, resulting in a wide range of flavors, from mild and fruity to bold and chocolaty.

How is coffee roasted?

Coffee roasting is a process that involves applying heat to green coffee beans to transform them into the roasted beans used for brewing coffee. The roasting process enhances the aroma, flavor, and color of the beans.

It's important to note that different roast levels, such as light, medium, or dark, can produce distinct flavors and aromas. The roasting process significantly influences the final taste and aroma of the coffee. Roasters often experiment with different roast profiles to achieve desired flavor profiles and to highlight the unique characteristics of different coffee varieties.

What is Latte Art?

Latte art is a creative and visually appealing technique used to decorate the surface of a latte or a coffee-based beverage. It involves the skilled pouring of steamed milk into a shot of espresso to create intricate and beautiful designs on the surface of the drink.

The process of creating latte art begins with the preparation of a shot of espresso, which serves as the base of the drink. Then, steamed milk is carefully poured into the espresso, and the barista uses various techniques to manipulate the milk and create patterns or designs.

The most common forms of latte art include the heart, tulip, rosetta, and swan designs. These designs are created by controlling the speed, angle, and height at which the milk is poured into the espresso. By carefully maneuvering the milk pitcher and making small adjustments with the wrist, the barista can form the desired shapes and patterns on the surface of the drink.

3 Types of Roast Level

Light Roast

Light roast coffee refers to coffee beans that have been roasted for a shorter period of time and at lower temperatures compared to medium or dark roast coffees.

Medium Roast

Medium roast coffee refers to coffee beans that have been roasted for a moderate amount of time, resulting in a balance between the flavors of the coffee bean and the roast itself.

Dark Roast

Dark roast coffee refers to coffee beans that have been roasted for a longer period of time and at higher temperatures, resulting in a darker brown to nearly black color.
